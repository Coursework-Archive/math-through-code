# Exponent Rules (Quick Reference)

These rules apply for any **nonzero base** `a`.

---

## 1. Negative Exponents (Reciprocals)

A negative exponent means **take the reciprocal**.

**Rule**
- $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$


**Examples**
- $3^{-4} = 1 / 81$
- $(-3)^{(-4)} = 1 / 81$

---

## 2. Parentheses vs. No Parentheses

Parentheses determine whether the negative sign is part of the base.

**Rule**
- $(-a)^{n} ≠ -a^{n}$ 

**Examples**
- $3^{-4} = 1 / 81$
- $-3^{-4} = -(3^{4}) = -81$

---

## 3. Even vs. Odd Powers

- Even powers → result is **positive**
- Odd powers → result keeps the **original sign**

**Examples**
- $(-2)^{4} = 16$
- $(-2)^{3} = -8$

---

## 4. Product Rule (Same Base)

**Rule**
- $(a)^{m} · (a)^{n} = (a)^{(m + n)}$

**Example**
- $(3)^{2} · (3)^{3} = (3)^{5}$

---

## 5. Quotient Rule (Same Base)

**Rule**
- $(a)^{m} / (a)^{n} = (a)^{(m - n)}$

**Example**
- $3^{5} / 3^{2} = 3^{3}$

---

## 6. Power of a Power

**Rule**
- $(a^{m})^{n} = a^{(m · n)}$

**Example**
- $(3^{2})^{4} = 3^{8}$

---
## 7. Power of a Quotient (different bases)

**Rule**
- $(a/b)^{n} = a^{n}/b^{n}$       

**Example**
- $(3/2)^{2} = 9/4$

---

## 8. Rational Exponent Rule

**Rule**
- $(a)^{(m/n)} = (\sqrt[n]a)^{m} = \sqrt[n]{a^m}$       

**Example**
- $16^{3/4} = \sqrt[4](16)^{3} = 2^{3} = 8$

---

## 9. Zero Exponent

**Rule**
- $a^{0} = 1$       
- $(a ≠ 0)$


**Example**
- $5^{0} = 1$

---
